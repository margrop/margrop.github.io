1. Racing category

In racing events, the order in which athletes pass the finish line is used as the main criterion to determine the ranking of athletes. The main racing events include: track and field (track events), swimming, cycling, rowing, canoeing, speed skating, short track speed skating, etc.

Collect content

  1. The time the athlete completes the game, accurate to milliseconds
  2. Competition event information, athlete information, athlete appearance order, athlete participation status (participation or abstention), number of athlete fouls, competition results, rankings

4. Confrontation (except ball games)

Confrontation events are generally completed by two teams or two athletes, and the winning or losing relationship between the two sides is used as the main criterion for judging the ranking of athletes. The characteristic of confrontation events is that both sides of the competition directly participate in the confrontation, and the audience can judge the results of most events by themselves. Due to the large number of ball games, the large number of participants and the complex rules, ball games are classified separately here. Confrontation events (except ball games) mainly include: wrestling, judo, taekwondo, boxing, martial arts, Sanshou, fencing, etc.

Collect content

  1. Game time and rest time between games
  2. Competition event information, both teams and player information, competition rounds, number of fouls, and scores

Ball games can be divided into big balls and small balls. Except for football, basketball, and volleyball, the three most popular ball games around the world, which are called big balls, all other ball games are called small balls. This article classifies the three big balls separately, and the remaining small balls into one category.
